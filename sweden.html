Sweden

From Swedish pilots guarding neutral skies in wooden Mosquito night fighters, to the pioneering supersonic double-delta J35 Drakens of the Cold War, to today's highly adaptable JAS 39 Gripen patrols — Swedish military aviation spans a century of self-reliance and unique aerospace innovation.

The revolutionary delta wing

Saab JAS 39 Gripen
"The agile backbone of Swedish NATO air defence"

A Swedish multirole fighter developed to defend against superior forces, produced by SAAB since 1988. It is designed to take off from highways and be ready for combat in minutes by a small team of mechanics. Users include Sweden, the Czech Republic, Hungary, South Africa, and Brazil.
