First French delta wing

A French supersonic fighter produced by Dassault from 1978 to 2007. Despite its older single-engine delta-wing design, it is a highly maneuverable machine that has proven itself in many conflicts. Today, it is being gradually replaced by the more modern Rafale. Its users include France, Greece, India, Taiwan, the UAE, and Peru.

Multirole Fighter
Rafale
"The agile backbone of Czech NATO air defence"

A French multirole 4.5-generation fighter aircraft with a characteristic delta wing, produced by Dassault Aviation since 1986. It exists in versions for both the Air Force and the Navy (M version). It is designed as a complete replacement for all older types of French fighters. Users include France, India, Egypt, Qatar, Greece, and Croatia.
