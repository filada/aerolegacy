Italy

From the pioneering days of the Regia Aeronautica and Macchi fighters over the Mediterranean in World War II, to Cold War jet designs like the Fiat G.91, to today’s Eurofighter and F-35 patrols safeguarding NATO airspace — Italian military aviation reflects a century of innovation, speed, and national pride.

Multi-role aircraft
Fiat G91
"GINA"

An Italian light jet attack aircraft produced by Fiat Aviazione from 1956 to 1977. It won a NATO competition for a standard light strike machine and was valued for its ability to operate from unprepared field airstrips. Users include Italy, Germany, Portugal, and Greece.
