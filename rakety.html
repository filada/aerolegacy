What is a Fox Call?
FOX 1
Semi-Active Radar Homing
SARH

The launching aircraft must continuously illuminate the target with its own radar throughout the missile's flight. If the shooter breaks the radar lock — the missile goes blind. Effective at medium range but requires the pilot to maintain a predictable flight path, limiting evasive maneuvers.

FOX 2
Infrared Homing
IR / Heat-Seeking

Locks onto the heat signature of an enemy aircraft's engines — no radar needed. Early Fox 2 missiles were rear-aspect only, requiring a tail-chase. Modern all-aspect IR missiles can attack from any angle, including head-on. Fire-and-forget: once launched, the shooter is free to maneuver.

FOX 3
Active Radar Homing
ARH / Fire and Forget

The missile carries its own active radar seeker. After launch, the aircraft provides mid-course guidance updates, but once the missile's onboard radar activates — it hunts independently. The launching aircraft can immediately turn away, engage other targets, or defend itself. Dominates beyond-visual-range combat.

AIM-7 Sparrow
The Workhorse

The Western world's standard medium-range air-to-air missile for three decades. Saw extensive combat from Vietnam to the Gulf War. Its SARH guidance demanded the pilot hold a radar lock throughout the engagement — a dangerous proposition in the heat of modern air combat.

AIM-9 Sidewinder
The Rattlesnake

One of the most produced and widely used air-to-air missiles in history. First fired in anger during the 1958 Taiwan Strait crisis. Evolved through dozens of variants over 70 years — the latest AIM-9X Block II features a helmet-mounted sight, all-aspect seeker, and off-boresight capability up to 90°.

03
Active Radar Homing
FOX 3 Missiles

The kings of beyond-visual-range combat. These weapons carry their own onboard radar — once active, the launching aircraft can disengage entirely. Modern BVR combat is shaped entirely around the Fox 3 threat.

FOX 3 · 001
FOX 3 · ARH
AIM-120 AMRAAM
Slammer

The gold standard of Western BVR missiles and the most widely deployed Fox 3 weapon in the world. Carried by virtually every Western fighter since the 1990s. The AIM-120D variant extends range beyond 160 km and adds two-way data link for in-flight target updates. First combat kill in 1992 over Iraq.

FOX 3 · ARH
Meteor
The Game Changer

Widely considered the most capable air-to-air missile in service today. Unlike all other Fox 3 missiles which use a solid-fuel rocket motor, the Meteor uses a ramjet — allowing it to sustain speed throughout its flight. The result is a 'no-escape zone' three times larger than the AMRAAM's, fundamentally changing BVR combat calculus.
