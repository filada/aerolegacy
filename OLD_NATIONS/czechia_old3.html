01
Single-seat Fighter
Supermarine Spitfire
"The most beautiful aircraft ever built"

When Czechoslovakia fell in 1939, hundreds of Czech airmen escaped to Britain and joined the Royal Air Force. They flew Spitfires with distinction in the Battle of Britain — earning more decorations per capita than any other Allied nation. The Spitfire's elliptical wing and Merlin engine made it the equal of anything the Luftwaffe could field.

02
Jet Trainer / Light Attack
Aero L-39 Albatros
"The most widely used jet trainer in history"

Designed and built entirely in Czechoslovakia, the L-39 Albatros became the standard jet trainer for Warsaw Pact air forces from the 1970s onwards. Over 2,900 were manufactured at the Aero Vodochody plant near Prague. It trained a generation of pilots across more than 30 countries — a remarkable achievement for a small nation behind the Iron Curtain.

03
Multirole Fighter
Saab JAS 39 Gripen
"The agile backbone of Czech NATO air defence"

After joining NATO in 1999, the Czech Republic leased 14 JAS 39 Gripen C/D fighters from Sweden. Lightweight, supersonic, and equipped with modern AESA radar and beyond-visual-range missiles, the Gripen marks a decisive break from Soviet-era MiGs. Czech Gripens regularly perform NATO Quick Reaction Alert duties over the Baltic region.
